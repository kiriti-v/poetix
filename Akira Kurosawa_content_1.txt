Akira Kurosawa, a renowned Japanese director and screenwriter, is considered one of the most influential filmmakers in cinema history. He directed over 30 movies throughout his career, including classics like Rashomon, Seven Samurai, and Yojimbo. Kurosawa's work has influenced directors such as Martin Scorsese and Francis Ford Coppola. After his death, a book titled "A Dream is a Genius" was published, which included a list of Kurosawa's 100 favorite films. The list includes films by directors such as Griffith, Lang, Chaplin, Hitchcock, Fellini, and Coppola.
