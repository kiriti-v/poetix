Francis Ford Coppola, the renowned director of films like The Godfather and Apocalypse Now, has shared his top 10 favorite films of all time. The list includes Ashes And Diamonds (1958) by Andrzej Wajda, The Best Years Of Our Lives (1946) by William Wyler, I Vitelloni (1953) by Federico Fellini, The Bad Sleep Well (1960) and Yojimbo (1961) by Akira Kurosawa, Singin' In The Rain (1952) by Stanley Donen and Gene Kelly, The King Of Comedy (1983) and Raging Bull (1980) by Martin Scorsese, The Apartment (1960) by Billy Wilder, and Sunrise (1927) by F.W. Murnau. Coppola has always been influenced by his peers and believes in giving his all to filmmaking.
