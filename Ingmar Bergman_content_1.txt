Ingmar Bergman, a renowned Swedish director, is considered one of the most influential filmmakers of all time. He directed over 60 films and documentaries, including Fanny and Alexander and The Virgin Spring. Martin Scorsese and Philip French have praised Bergman's impact and legacy. Bergman himself was influenced by fellow filmmaker Andrei Tarkovsky. In 1994, Bergman listed his 11 favorite films, which include Andrei Rublev, The Circus, The Passion of Joan of Arc, and Sunset Boulevard. Bergman believed that film and music have a strong emotional connection and that both art forms affect our emotions directly. He aimed to tell the truth about the human condition through his films.
