Steven Spielberg, the renowned director, has compiled a list of his 20 favorite movies of all time. Some of the notable titles on his list include "It's a Wonderful Life" by Frank Capra, "The Godfather" by Francis Ford Coppola, "Fantasia" by Walt Disney, "Psycho" by Alfred Hitchcock, "2001: A Space Odyssey" by Stanley Kubrick, "Lawrence of Arabia" by David Lean, "Citizen Kane" by Orson Welles, and "Seven Samurai" by Akira Kurosawa. Spielberg has always been influenced by other filmmakers and pays homage to them in his work. He has been active in the film industry for over 50 years and has won the Best Director award at the Academy Awards twice.
