Ingmar Bergman, the renowned director, had a list of his favorite films. Some of these films include "The Phantom Carriage" and "The Last Laugh" by Victor Sjöström, "Sunrise: A Song of Two Humans" and "Faust" by F.W. Murnau, "The Passion of Joan of Arc" by Carl Theodor Dreyer, "The Circus" by Charlie Chaplin, "Rashômon" by Akira Kurosawa, "Psycho" by Alfred Hitchcock, "La Dolce Vita" and "La Strada" by Federico Fellini, and "Andrei Rublev" by Andrei Tarkovsky. Bergman also admired the works of Michelangelo Antonioni, Vilgot Sjöman, and Thomas Vinterberg, among others.
