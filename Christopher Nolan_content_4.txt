Director Christopher Nolan has named his 10 favorite movies of all time. They include "Heat" (1995) directed by Michael Mann, "Blade Runner" (1984) directed by Ridley Scott, "Metropolis" (1927) directed by Fritz Lang, "The Testament of Dr. Mabuse" (1933) also directed by Fritz Lang, "2001: A Space Odyssey" (1968) directed by Stanley Kubrick, "The Right Stuff" (1983) directed by Philip Kaufman, "Speed" (1994) directed by Jan de Bont, "Chariots of Fire" (1981) directed by Hugh Hudson, and "Lawrence of Arabia" (1962) directed by David Lean. These films have influenced Nolan's approach to filmmaking and have inspired him in various ways.
